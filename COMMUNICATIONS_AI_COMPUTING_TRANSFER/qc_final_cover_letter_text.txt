Dear Editors,
We are pleased to submit “PGAA: distribution-aware ranking of heterogeneous
single-cell perturbation responses” for consideration as an Article in Communications AI & Computing.
PGAA addresses a practical computational problem in single-cell perturbation
analysis: ranking heterogeneous responses that are not well summarized by
mean-shift tests. It integrates PGAA-W, a Wasserstein-based distributional
ranking score, PGAA-H, a guarded histogram-shape diagnostic, permutation
calibration, and a reproducible Python/R implementation with a CLI into
a transparent ranking component for hypothesis generation. Adamson 2016
UPR CRISPRi provides proof-of-principle evidence (5 perturbations, 13 positives); Norman 2019 CRISPRa provides a PGAA-W extension across three
perturbations (KLF1, CEBPE, CEBPA) and a focused PGAA-H case study
(ELANE/PRTN3 in CEBPE; BHLHE40 AUROC 0.833). SCEPTRE is compared directly on Norman CEBPE. Observational datasets are retained only
as marker-recovery stress checks. Broader systematic distributional-test benchmarking and genome-scale Perturb-seq evaluation are acknowledged as future
work.
