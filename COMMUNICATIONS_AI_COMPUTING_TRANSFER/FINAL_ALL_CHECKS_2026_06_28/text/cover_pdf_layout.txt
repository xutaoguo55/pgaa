Dear Editors,
We are pleased to submit our manuscript, “A distribution-aware computational framework for
prioritizing heterogeneous responses in single-cell perturbation data,” for consideration as an
Article in Communications AI & Computing.
The manuscript presents PGAA as a computational framework for prioritizing heterogeneous
transcriptional responses in single-cell perturbation data. PGAA combines PGAA-W
Wasserstein for full-expression-distribution shifts with a PGAA-H histogram-shape diagnostic
for responder-associated expression-shape changes, both accompanied by permutation
calibration and Storey upper-tail diagnostics. The framework is implemented in Python and R as
a reproducible ranking layer for perturbation screens and marker-anchored single-cell contrasts.
The evidence is organized around computational scope, benchmarking, and reproducibility.
Adamson 2016 UPR CRISPRi provides a small independent proof-of-principle benchmark.
Norman 2019 CRISPRa provides a multi-perturbation PGAA-W extension across KLF1,
CEBPE, and CEBPA, plus a narrow CEBPE PGAA-H ranking example for ELANE. A
processed-source-data MMD-PSM comparator is included as an additional distribution-aware
baseline. Observational disease-related scRNA-seq datasets are retained as marker-recovery
stress checks, not as causal disease validation or clinical biomarker evidence.
We believe the work fits Communications AI & Computing because it addresses a general
computational problem in modern single-cell analysis: how to prioritize response programs when
perturbation effects are heterogeneous and not adequately summarized by mean shifts. The
manuscript provides calibration guardrails, simulation ablations, software availability, and a
supplementary reproducibility archive.
We do not claim new clinical biomarkers, treatment recommendations, or bedside diagnostic
utility. The public code-only repository is available at https://github.com/xutaoguo55/pgaa and is
archived at Zenodo (DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20681141) and Software Heritage.
Manuscript-specific source-data tables, figure-reproduction scripts, runtime documentation, and
PDF build files are provided in the supplementary software archive.
